#Abstract:
While Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate high proficiency in retrieving and summarizing static regulatory texts, their ability to maintain compliance during multi-step autonomous task execution (Agentic workflows) remains under-evaluated. Current benchmarks like MMLU or GSM8K focus on general reasoning rather than domain-specific constraints. This project introduces a Compliance-Aware Evaluation Framework inspired by HealthBench (Kratsios et al., 2024), specifically tailored for the banking sector. We analyze how agents fail to uphold PII (Personally Identifiable Information) protection and anti-money laundering (AML) protocols when faced with adversarial "social engineering" prompts, despite "knowing" the underlying regulations.

#Key Citations:

Kratsios et al. (2024), HealthBench: Rigorous Evaluation for Healthcare AI: "General-purpose benchmarks fail to capture the high-stakes failure modes of domain-specific AI."

Nair et al. (2023), Derisking AI in Financial Services: "The gap between policy knowledge and behavioral alignment is the primary hurdle for financial AI deployment."
